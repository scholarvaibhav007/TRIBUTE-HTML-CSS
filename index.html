The Missile Man Of India:


Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam BR (15 October 1931 â€“ 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. Born and raised in a Muslim family in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he studied physics and aerospace engineering. Over four decades, he worked as a scientist and science administrator, primarily at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), and was deeply involved in India's civilian space program and military missile development efforts.

For his work on ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology, he became known as the Missile Man of India. He also played a crucial role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998.

Kalam was elected president in 2002 with support from both the Bharatiya Janata Party and the Indian National Congress. Often referred to as the "People's President," he returned to a life of education, writing, and public service after his presidency. He received several prestigious awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor.

Kalam passed away on 27 July 2015, after collapsing from an apparent cardiac arrest while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. He was 83. His funeral in Rameswaram was attended by thousands, including national dignitaries, and he was buried with full state honors.
